OUR ROBOTS

Education Robots

Robots for school education as innovative resources in the classroom and technology for students with learning disabilities. They are extra eyes and ears for playground safety, pool safety, and child care. They educate in many languages, providing entertainment and learning.

Disability Robots

Robots are available for people of all ages with practically all disabilities. They provide Assistive Technology to the elderly, to those challenged by language and speech barriers, to the physically challenged, those with mobility impairment, and social disabilities. There are robots to assist in communication, robots to assist with activities of daily living and robots to entertain.

Aged care/child care Robots

Robots in aged care settings to assist our elderly loved ones with activities of daily living or robots to entertain those living in aged care facilities. To allow access to remote health services or help with cleaning. Loneliness is a crippling social issue that has been met by companion robots that enliven the spirit and communicate with voice, video and general conversation.

Medical robots

Not only are robots at the cutting edge in surgical accuracy, preventing surgical errors for thousands of patients, robots also disinfect, detect temperatures, pacify patients, deliver medical supplies from room to room, lift, patients, and provide clinical training. They also initiate safe check-ins, collect essential medical information, provide surveys, and both welcome and entertain patients.

Domestic robots

Robots are being used throughout homes right now, helping with domestic chores, such as vacuuming and washing floors, they can disinfect, provide surveillance, entertainment, offer video connection of family and friends, they keep good company, they tell jokes, greet guests, and can deliver reminders or messages. As children grow up surrounded by technology, they quickly learn and avail themselves to the emerging IoT world where everything will interact. Robots in the home can interact with smart home technologies, such as Alexa and Google, and will be an integral part of our daily lives as refrigerators and televisions have become.

Social robots

A social robot is designed to interact with humans, or even with other robots. In a workplace setting, they can greet customers and offer basic customer services by answering questions or offering directions. Humans may find it challenging to greet clients all day long while standing at a door; this prolonged standing can strain the legs and put pressure on the back and spine. Robots, on the other hand, never tire of monotonous tasks. They are always cheerful, always ready to entertain and offer humour, and they are capable of many tasks and greeting in many languages.

Telepresence robots

A telepresence robot is able to stand-in for an operator so as to engage with customers from a distance. It can be operated by a healthcare consultant or place a surgeon in a remote location. It can be a night watchman, or a concierge. It allows a person to engage with the operator in a variety of ways. Telepresence robots can place an operator at the bottom of the sea, or in a dangerous chemical environment, or even in space. They are also ideal for teleconferencing or training staff.

Companion robots

A companion robot is a robotic friend or companion. They not only keep you company, they have significant benefits for your emotional and physical health. They have been known to raise levels of neurotransmitters that are depleted through loneliness and boredom. By interacting with your companion robot, the elderly, children, disabled and lonely have all gained valuable meaning and purpose in caring for, and being cared by, a loveable life-like pet or robotic carer that creates joy and happiness for thousands of people around the world.

Service robots

The tasks that service robots carry out are classified into different categories from dangerous or repetitious jobs to house cleaning duties. In a workplace, a hospital, a restaurant, a workshop, or an industrial facility, they can deliver goods. These robots can be used in policing, security or traffic control. They automate many of the most basic of tasks in customer service. Other tasks these robots can perform include check-in for appointments, tech support, registrations for events, and multiple other applications for the home and business.
